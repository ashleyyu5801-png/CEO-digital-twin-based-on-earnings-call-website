01 / Introduction

About This Project

We examine whether a large language model can learn and reproduce the distinctive disclosure and conversational style of a high-profile CEO.

Earnings calls are a rich source of data that captures individual-level managerial attributes. During these calls, executives discuss financial results, answer analyst questions, and reveal their communication patterns, values, and strategic thinking.

Our digital twin of Jamie Dimon is trained on his historical earnings call transcripts to predict his responses to analyst questions in subsequent calls. We evaluate the generated answers against real answers across three dimensions: semantic similarity, informational accuracy, and tone alignment.

02 / Approach

Methodology

We designed four specifications that vary along three dimensions to compare different approaches to digital twin construction.

01

Random 500 Q&A

Randomly samples 500 Q&A pairs from historical data as in-context examples.

  • Broad temporal coverage
  • Representative sampling
  • Topic diversity
Baseline
02

Recent 500 Q&A

Uses the 500 most recent Q&A pairs, sorted by date in descending order.

  • Current communication style
  • Recent market context
  • Temporal relevance
Recency Focus
03

Recent Q&A + 10-Q

Extends Spec 2 by prepending quarterly 10-Q financial summary before examples.

  • Financial context
  • Factual grounding
  • Specific metrics
Data Augmented
04

Persona + 10-Q

Uses a compressed persona summary extracted from Q&A history plus 10-Q data.

  • Explicit persona modeling
  • Token efficient
  • Scalable approach
Best Performing

Qualitative Human Analysis

Manual review of generated vs. real responses reveals systematic patterns in how the digital twin diverges from Jamie Dimon's actual communication style.

‚úó

Vague & High-Level

Generated answers often give deflective responses that don't directly answer the question, unlike Dimon's specific replies.

‚úó

Excessive Politeness

Model starts with filler phrases like "Thanks for the question" and "Certainly" which Dimon rarely uses.

‚úó

Sanitized Tone

Model uses corporate-speak while Dimon is often blunt, saying things like "stupid CECL" or "deep flaws."

‚úó

Over-Hedging

Model uses "might," "could," "potentially" frequently, whereas Jamie makes definitive statements.

‚úó

Missing Numbers

Model avoids specific numbers and factual details, giving safer, more general explanations.

‚úó

Verbose Responses

Generated answers are typically longer and less concise than Dimon's direct, to-the-point replies.

Key Takeaways

01

High Semantic Fidelity

The model excels at capturing the executive's conversational style and general semantic framing, with similarity scores averaging 0.8.

02

Limited Detail Reproduction

Significant limitations in reproducing informational accuracy‚Äîthe model often misses specific details as indicated by modest NLI entailment rates.

03

Tone Gap

The model struggles to replicate the CEO's subtle tone alignment, particularly his characteristic bluntness and directness.

04

Persona Approach Works

The Persona Summary + 10-Q specification demonstrates an efficient, scalable approach that achieves comparable performance while significantly reducing token load.
